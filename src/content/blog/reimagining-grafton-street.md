Grafton Street has long been the beating heart of Dublin's retail and social life. But as cities around the world rethink their relationship with public space, it's worth asking: could we do more with this extraordinary street?

## The Current State

While Grafton Street is technically pedestrianised, it still feels like a corridor — a place you move *through* rather than a place you *stay in*. The street furniture is sparse, the planting is minimal, and there's little to invite you to linger.

## What Could Change

Drawing inspiration from successful projects in Copenhagen, Barcelona, and Melbourne, here are a few ideas:

### Better Seating

Not just benches, but a variety of seating options — moveable chairs, stepped seating areas, and sheltered spots for rainy days. People sit where there are places to sit.

### Green Infrastructure

Mature trees would provide shade and shelter, while rain gardens could manage stormwater runoff. Planters could double as informal seating.

### Wayfinding and Identity

A cohesive wayfinding system could connect Grafton Street to surrounding laneways and cultural venues, encouraging exploration beyond the main drag.

## Looking Ahead

The best public spaces evolve. They respond to the people who use them. Grafton Street deserves a design that matches the energy and ambition of the city it serves.
