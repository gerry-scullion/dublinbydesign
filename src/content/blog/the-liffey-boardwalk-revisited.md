The Liffey has always been Dublin's spine — the feature that gives the city its shape and its character. Yet for decades, the river was something Dubliners turned their backs on. The boardwalk was an attempt to change that.

## What Works

The basic idea is sound: a timber boardwalk at water level, bringing people closer to the river. On a sunny afternoon, it's one of the best spots in the city. The scale is right — wide enough to feel generous but narrow enough to feel intimate.

The lighting design deserves particular praise. The uplighting along the boardwalk creates a warm atmosphere after dark, and the views of the Ha'penny Bridge are genuinely beautiful.

## What Could Be Better

### Maintenance

The timber is showing its age in places. Some sections feel uneven underfoot, and several of the original light fittings have been replaced with mismatched alternatives.

### Activity

The boardwalk could support more activity — seasonal markets, pop-up food stalls, outdoor exhibitions. Right now, it's primarily a walking route, which underuses its potential.

### Connections

Getting onto and off the boardwalk isn't always intuitive. Better connections to surrounding streets and bridges would increase footfall and make it feel more integrated into the city's walking network.

## Lessons Learned

The boardwalk shows that Dublin is at its best when it embraces the Liffey. Every project along the quays should ask: how does this bring people closer to the water?
